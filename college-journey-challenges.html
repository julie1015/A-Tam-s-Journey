COLLEGE JOURNEY CHALLENGES!

In this section, the developers will share the challenges they faced while they are on the adjustment period of being a college student.

MICKEY

For me, starting college was a roller coaster of feelings and transitions.

I initially had trouble juggling my obligations and adjusting to the new setting.

The amount of work required for school was excessive. It was difficult for me to make new friends because I didn't know how to interact with people in such a strange environment.

Another element of hardship was the commute. I frequently felt exhausted from lack of sleep and scheduling constraints.

I overcame these obstacles by learning to keep going get stronger, and adapt to the rigours of college life.

ALLISON

During my college journey, I faced various challenges that tested my patience and adaptability.

One of the biggest hurdles was dealing with too many group projects, which often required managing different personalities and schedules.

Commuting daily was also exhausting, as traffic and long travel times drained my energy before and after classes.

Academic pressure, combined with strict deadlines, left me struggling to keep up with my studies and maintain good grades.

On top of it all, the lack of sleep due to late-night studying and project deadlines made it even harder to stay focused and motivated.

BEA

Starting college was really challenging for me at first. I was extremely shy and not used to commuting, so adjusting to this new routine was overwhelming during my first week.

Making friends was also nerve-wracking because I didn’t know where to start, but I’m really thankful for the people who naturally came into my life.

They’ve been a big support system for me, especially when I’m dealing with academic pressure or trying to learn new things.

One of the hardest parts of college has been managing all the deadlines. It feels impossible to keep up, especially during midterms and finals, when exams and projects seem to pile up all at once.

The constant lack of sleep, limited time, and low energy make it even more difficult to adjust to the demands of college life.

JULIE ANN

For me, being 3 hours away from college is the biggest challenge i had. It often leads to feelings of homesickness and isolation, making it tough to stay close to family.

I do encounter stressfull things, like tight schedule, and deadlines, but it is tougher when you need to always consider the hours you'll spend on traveling.

The cost and time of travel can be draining, especially when it impacts my studies and personal life.

I also miss out on family events and sometimes struggle with not being able to go home easily during difficult times.

Being far away makes it harder to participate in campus activities or seek immediate help when needed.
